Yoga Definition

Yoga is an ancient practice focused on breathing and physical exercises (asanas) to improve mental and physical well-being. It aligns the body and mind, addressing health issues, and the term "yoga," from Sanskrit, signifies the union of mind and body through breath.

Hatha Yoga is characterized by slower-paced classes where poses are held for multiple breaths. It highlights the connection between physical postures and breathing, with the goal of opening energy channels, especially in the spine, to promote the smooth flow of energy. It's worth noting that well-known yoga schools like Vinyasa, Ashtanga, Bikram, and Iyengar all originate from Hatha yoga.

Yin yoga focuses on extended, seated, or lying poses, focusing on flexibility, connective tissues, and promoting balance, stress relief, and improved flexibility. It offers a unique approach to joint health compared to active stretching.

Vinyasa, also known as "Baptiste Yoga," "Prana Flow," or "Power Yoga," emphasizes the integration of breath with smooth transitions between poses while following a consistent sequence in classes that enhances stamina.

Ashtanga yoga is an intensive practice featuring structured sequences performed in a precise order. It can be instructed in a guided class or a self-paced format, requiring students to commit the sequence and timing to memory. Ashtanga is known for its rigorous nature, discipline, and its focus on generating heat for detoxification and core strengthening.

"There is a yoga style practiced in a heated room that encourages significant sweating and loosening of muscles. This style can include various types, like Yin or Vinyasa, but it's specifically conducted in a hot environment. Due to this intensity, individuals with health issues and pregnant women are advised against practicing this type of yoga."
